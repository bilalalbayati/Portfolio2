Carp Fish Overview

Today I would like to talk about one of the specific kinds of fish called Carp. Carp are a large group of freshwater fish in the family Cyprinidae, a very large group of fish species. This group that is kept by aquarists includes species from Eastern Europe, namely common Carp and koi, mirror carp or comoragh Carp, goldfish, and the Indian Carp. The most common species kept by aquarists is the koi/mirror carp.

what is the Carp fish?

Carp fish is a type of freshwater fish. They live in the water. They eat plants that grow in the water, and they also need to breathe oxygen from the air at the surface of the water. Their natural instinct is to eat plants in lakes or ponds or slow-moving streams near lakes or ponds. When there are plants there, carp fish will stay near them for food. Carp Fish can also be found in saltwater, but it's not their natural habitat, so carp fish needs to go inland when it wants to spawn then go back into saltwater after it's done spawning. Carp fish eat mostly aquatic plants and small invertebrates such as insects and plankton. They can also consume zooplankton, which is made up of many small aquatic animals. Carp primarily feed on diatoms and algae. Their diet varies depending on the season and factors such as temperature, water flow, and amount of silt in the water.

Some carp species also consume detritus - organic debris that bacteria have broken down into simpler molecules for use as food by other organisms like worms, crustaceans, or plants. Carp are air breathers with no need to surface for air, so they spend most of their time at depths of 2-4 meters (6-13 feet).
